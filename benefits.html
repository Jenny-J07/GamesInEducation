Benefits

Team Work

By utilising games in your lessons, this can greatly improve children's teamworking skills. There are a range of games that allow for children to work in groups or pairs to complete tasks. Teamworking is an essential skill that can be used in all aspects of life.

This can also help teach children how to communicate effectively, resolve conflict and take on leadership roles if the game must be played in a larger group.

Problem Solving

Games are also a great resource to improve problem solving skills. Educational games can provide challenges for children to solve. This helps to build up their problem solving skills in a fun manner.

With so many games to choose from, there are no shortages of different puzzles for children to solve. This can be a very rewarding experience for children when they solve a problem.

Survival island games are a great example of a problem solving game that can be used to improve these skills.

Decision Making

Games can help children improve their decision making skills. This is because they are able to learn in a virtual environment where they can make mistakes without real consequences.

There are a wide range of games focused on the player taking the role of a business owner and making decisions to build up their company. Games like these offer children the chance to try out different decisions to see where it leads them. This can also help to inspire creativity through decisions.

Motivate and Engage Children

Games are a brilliant way of engaging students in topics they might otherwise find complicated or boring. Games that have an element of fun, with the right level of challenge can be a great way to engage children in their learning. These games also provide a more hands-on way of learning, which can help to motivate children that prefer to learn by doing.
